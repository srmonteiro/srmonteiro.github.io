Romantic Roots

In the 1800s, many US colleges and universities were founded establishing
their own traditions and musical cultures. Romantic era pieces were used
as the melody for alma maters which reveals that they were a part of oral
traditions in the US and sung widely along with popular and religious tunes.

I thought the trend to have the melodies of alma maters borrowed from Romantic music was more widespread across the country from my biased experience in the Midwest. My alma mater is the University of Michigan so I was most familiar “The Yellow and Blue” based off of Michael William Balfe’s The Enchantress. I had also frequently heard neighboring Michigan State University’s “Shadows” which resembles Donizetti’s Lucia di Lammermoor and Northwestern’s Hymn based on Haydn’s St. Anthony Chorale which Brahms wrote a variation based on. Another notable example is the University of Wisconsin’s “Varsity” from the tune of Gounod’s “Domine Salvum Fac” from the St. Cecilia Mass. These midwestern schools likely had more contact due to being geographically close which could account for a similar practice in setting the alma mater to music from a classical genre.

When looking at the alma maters I examined, I found borrowing the melody from classical music of the Romantic era was the fourth most common. Most alma maters were written in the late 19th/early 20th century and passed through an oral traditions and university musical organizations.

There are three main models for how alma maters came to be written 1. The Glee Club wanted a new song or began singing a song that was adopted as the alma mater 2. The students or administrators were inspired by another school’s alma mater 3. The University held a song contest.

The song contest model invited members of the university community at large to send in words for an alma mater (often with a reward) and it was set to music, most likely already in existence although sometimes later composed to fit the text. Typically, alma maters will mention key features of the school landscape or unique characteristics.

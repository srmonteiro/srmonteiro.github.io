“The Yellow and Blue” exemplifies many of the characteristics of a typical alma mater. However, the song’s unique traits make for a useful test case.

English Professor, Charles M. Gayley, wrote the text for “The Yellow and Blue” winning a song contest put on by the university. The text was set to the Pirate’s Chorus called “Ever Be Happy” from Michael William Balfe’s The Enchantress.

Michael William Balfe was one of the most popular composers of English-language opera. English language opera was popular in the US throughout the 1860s and 70s. Katherine K. Preston explores English-language opera companies that were prominent in the post-bellum South performing primarily European repertoire translated into English. In her book Opera for the People, she argues that despite popular belief that opera was reserved for social elites, at the time, people of what we would now call middle class were enjoying English language opera regularly.

Annie Lisle

The melody of “Annie Lisle” was the most commonly used in my data set. It is believed to have first
been used for Cornell’s alma mater, “Far Above Cayuga’s Waters” in 1870. Despite its widespread use
as the tune of many high school and university alma maters, little has been written about the composer, H.S. Thompson

H.S. Thompson was born in Massachusetts in 1825 and was an active performer, teacher, and composer. He was connected with minstrel groups in Boston and St. Louis. Perhaps his connection to minstrelsy is a reason that little is known about him. He has appeared in some census reports, but there are few written accounts regarding Thompson. His biographical information was investigated by James Joyce scholars because another one of his songs, “Lily Dale,” is quoted in A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man in 1916. This shows that his music was widespread and popular well into the 20th century

“Annie Lisle” was popular during the time Cornell was founded in 1865 and students were likely singing around campus which made it easily adopted to the tune of the alma mater. In my data set, the University of Kansas was the first to use “Annie Lisle” for the alma mater after Cornell in 1891 and was quickly followed by Syracuse University and Indiana University in 1893. Since “Annie Lisle” remained a popular tune, it is hard to tell if other universities were inspired by Cornell’s alma mater or set the tune independently. However, based on similar themes in the text, it would seem that Cornell’s song was well known and enviable for an alma mater. Alma maters set to this tune typically start by introducing the landscape of the university and include the word “hail” in the refrain

University of Georgia


From the hills of Georgia's northland
Beams thy noble brow,
And the sons of Georgia rising
Pledge with sacred vow.
'Neath the pine trees' stately shadow
Spread thy riches rare.
And thy sons, dear Alma Mater,
Will thy treasures share.
And thy daughters proudly join thee,
Take their rightful place,
Side by side into the future,
Equal dreams embrace.
Through the ages, Alma Mater,
Men will look to thee;
Thou the fairest of the Southland,
Georgia's Varsity.
(Chorus)
Alma Mater, thee we'll honor,
True and loyal be,
Ever crowned with praise and glory,

Annie Lisle


Down where the waving willows
’Neath the sunbeams smile,
Shadow’d o’er the murm’ring waters
Dwelt sweet Annie Lisle;
Pure as the forest lily,
Never tho’t of guile
Had its home within the bosom
Of sweet Annie Lisle.


Wave willows, murmur waters,
Golden sunbeams, smile!
Earthly music cannot waken
Lovely Annie Lisle.


Sweet came the hallow’d chiming
Of the Sabbath bell,
Borne on the morning breezes
Down the woody dell.
On a bed of pain and anguish
Lay dear Annie Lisle,
Chang’d were the lovely features,
Gone the happy smile.


Wave willows, murmur waters,
Golden sunbeams, smile!
Earthly music cannot waken
Lovely Annie Lisle.

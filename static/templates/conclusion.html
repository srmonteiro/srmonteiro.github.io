Conclusion

As evidenced in the test cases and data, an effective alma mater has a familiar melody and evokes nostalgia for the college years. The tradition of alma maters has persisted at least in part as a way to fuel sentimental attachments to the university that lead to major gift donations. In an article on Harvard’s alma mater, “ Fair Harvard”, the band director explains how the band acts as a “spirit unit” at alumni events and “encourages alumni to raise song”—not to mention give money—to Harvard. Alma maters are used as a way to engage alumni and raise funds. Princeton’s alma mater displays dedication to the university proclaiming “our hearts will give while we shall live.” A compelling way to collect funds, alma maters take alumni back to their college days.

What happens when the alma mater from the college days is unfamiliar? Does it have the same effect?

Like many other institutions, Roosevelt University hosted a contest for an alma mater to be written for the school. A text was chosen and a composition faculty member wrote the accompanying music. This is common practice for many alma maters around the late 19th early 20th century. However, for Roosevelt University, this process took place in 2003.

A winner was chosen and then composition faculty member, Stacy Garrop, wrote the music. The alma mater was to be premiered at the upcoming graduation ceremony. Although, it appears many alumni were unaware of the alma mater even nine years later when the University posted about it on facebook. It is relatively difficult to find a recording of the song online and the only copy of the score I could find is for in library viewing only at Roosevelt.

One reason the Roosevelt University alma mater may have been a flop is because of its large scoring. Garrop wrote the piece for full choir and orchestra scored the winds in two distinct parts. It seems that it was written primarily for commencement ceremonies in which the school’s orchestra would be provided. However, with such a large score and a format that would be expensive to recreate externally, and therefore, the piece does not have versatility. A piano reduction with single vocal melody, for example, would have helped for the song to be distributed or learned across the University community.

Now Roosevelt University has a song to fill its official university ceremonies. Although, it is questionable who can sing along. If in its most noble, an alma mater inspires nostalgia for the college experience, why create a new one in the 21st century?

What is the role of the alma mater today?

From personal experience, sometimes the only students who know the words to the alma mater are those involved with vocal organizations, glee clubs, or pep bands. Although these songs are not always of the highest artistic quality, alma maters provide a lens to look at a rare tradition that has roots from over 100 years ago. This website has defined characteristics of the genre and outlined how alma maters came to exist. I hope to expand this research further to determine the ways alma maters persist as living traditions on campuses today. Visitors are encouraged to submit their experiences with alma maters and add to the growing data set.
